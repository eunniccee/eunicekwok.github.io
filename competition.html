Design Hackathons


IDEATE!2021 - Winner

Designed a Virtual Reality simulation called WorkGround during this 48-Hours Ideathon to encourage better work-life harmony in workplaces especially during the current work-from-home situation. My team successfully clinched the first place among 200 other participants.

Healthcare Innovation Programme - Winner

Ideated and improved the existing Pilbox system supported by Polyclinics to make collecting medications more accessible and convenient for the patients yet ensure the various medications are always in the optimal environment. Although it was a short 8 hours Ideathon, our team managed to come up with innovative solutions and immerged as the winner among the 8 other groups.

SUTD Sustainable Design Hackathon 2022

Tackling the topic of Urban Farming, my group decided to design an all-in-one smart planter, HerbGround, to make micro-agriculture more readily available, convenient, cheaper, with smaller footprints, and more productive in homes and schools. We successfully immerged as the top 5 teams who made it to the Finals.

More details on this project can be found here!

Sports Competition



Judo '18-'19

I am happy to be able to build up my confidence through this sport where not only do I step out of my comfort zone, but also learn to be more courageous. Although most of us came in with zero experience, we are proud to see how much we have grown in such a short time.

Here are the competitions I've represented the school in throughout the short 1.5 years in Nanyang.

  • National School Games (A'Division)
  • Jagsport Invitational Competition
  • Judo Taishan Warrior Challenge (Groundwork Competition)

    • During the Jagsport Invitational Competition, I was fortunante to be able to learn, train, and compete with some of the Indonesia and Sri Lanka national players.

    Netball '14-'17

    Being a member of a team sport has taught me the importance of teamwork and trust. I am grateful to be part of a bonded family where everyone takes care of one another. As my coach always says, a rainbow without 7 colors won't be called a rainbow. Every color plays an important role and this applies to Netball too. It is important to tap on each member's strengths to complement their weaknesses. I am glad to receive such a meaningful and fruitful experience since young, building the team player characteristic.

    I was selected among 22 members to be one of the 12 to represent the school for both B'Division and C'Division.
