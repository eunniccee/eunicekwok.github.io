Community Service


Student Mentor

Under Youth Engagement At Heartlands (YEAH!) Programme, I volunteered as a student mentor every Friday at Tampines West CC to assist 4-5 primary school students with their work and lead sports activities & science experiments. We also played a crucial role in ensuring we provide appropriate advice, and be someone supportive whom they can befriend and trust.

Student Teacher

As part of the Overseas Community Involvement Programme (OCIP), we conducted virtual educational lessons for both primary and secondary school students in Vietnam (Eco Vietnam Group).
My group focussed on the topic of biodiversity as well as the importance of sustainable practices. Through games and quizzes, we interacted very well with the students and had a memorable experience!

Community Volunteer

Signed up as a member of East Youths where I get to be actively involved in biweekly befriending sessions with the elderly living in Chai Chee, and conduct interviews with less fortunate residents to find out the exact needs they would like the organization to help with.


This allows me to

  • Empathize and have a better awareness of the types of problems faced
  • Spent quality time with them to keep them happy and occupied
  • Provide appropriate and beneficial resources & assistance to less fortunate families

    • I am also a member of the Welfare Team where we plan bonding activities for the organization

    Silver Homes

    During my time in both Nanyang JC and SUTD, I've participated in this home improvement initiative where we hope to improve the mental and physical well-being of seniors. Such an activity enables me to leverage my empathetic and compassionate heart to help brighten up the day of the elderly as well as improve their living conditions. I have since become more empathetic toward the elderly and gained a better understanding of the challenges they faced through our conversations.

    Food Distribution

    As part of East Youths, we also have monthly food distribution event where we collect over 400 foods from other residents to distribute to less fortunate families. Over 60 families across 3 different blocks in Chai Chee have benefited from our event each time.
